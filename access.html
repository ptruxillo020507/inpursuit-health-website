The CMS ACCESS Model — How You Get Paid

It really is this simple.

1
You have patients
Your Medicare patients with chronic conditions — diabetes, hypertension, CKD, chronic pain, depression — already qualify.
2
CMS pays you monthly
For each enrolled patient, CMS sends a monthly Outcome-Aligned Payment. No visit required. The check comes every month.
3
Your visit revenue stays
ACCESS is purely additive. Your existing fee-for-service revenue from office visits, E&M, and procedures doesn't change.
Eligible patients  ×  ~$47/month  ×  12 months  =  new annual revenue
Example: 4,000 patients × $47 × 12 = $2.3M/year in new money from CMS — on top of what you already earn.

That's the whole model. CMS pays you to manage chronic conditions you're already managing. The only thing standing between you and that revenue is the health IT infrastructure to participate — which is exactly what InPursuit provides, at zero upfront cost.

Questions

How InPursuit turns the model into actual revenue.

How does InPursuit increase Annual Wellness Visit (AWV) completion?
Most practices complete AWVs for only 25-30% of their Medicare panel — not because patients don't qualify, but because no one is proactively scheduling them. TETRA identifies every Medicare patient due for an AWV, generates automated outreach (calls, texts, patient portal messages), and flags them at check-in so your front desk never misses one. Practices using proactive AWV workflows consistently hit 45-60% completion rates — nearly doubling revenue from a visit you're already equipped to deliver.
How does InPursuit scale Chronic Care Management (CCM) enrollment?
CCM pays $62/month per enrolled patient, but most practices only enroll 5% of eligible patients because the administrative burden is crushing — consent forms, 20-minute monthly touchpoints, documentation, billing. TETRA automates the enrollment workflow: identifies patients with 2+ chronic conditions, generates consent, assigns care coordination tasks to your team (or ours in Full Service), tracks the 20-minute clinical time requirement, and submits 99490/99491 claims automatically. Target: 15-25% enrollment of your chronic panel.
How does InPursuit make Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) work at scale?
RPM is one of the highest-revenue programs ($84/month effective) but most practices enroll under 2% because managing devices and data is a full-time job. TETRA integrates with connected devices (BP cuffs, glucose monitors, pulse oximeters), automatically collects 16+ days of readings per month (the CMS billing threshold), flags abnormal values for clinical review, and generates the documentation for 99453-99458 billing. Your clinician reviews alerts — TETRA handles everything else. Target: 10-18% of your chronic panel on active RPM.
How do we hit the 50% Outcome Attainment Threshold to earn full ACCESS payment?
CMS requires that at least 50% of your aligned patients meet their OAP Measure targets (e.g., 15mmHg BP reduction, HbA1c control) to earn 100% of payments. Below 50%, payment reduces proportionally. TETRA tracks every patient's progress against their specific targets in real time. A dashboard shows you exactly where each patient stands — green (on track), yellow (at risk), red (needs intervention). Care gap alerts fire automatically when a patient is trending toward missing their target, so your team can intervene with medication adjustments, follow-up visits, or care coordination before the assessment period ends.
What does InPursuit actually do vs. what does my practice do?
You deliver patient care. That's it. InPursuit handles: ACCESS application preparation, TETRA platform deployment, EHR integration (or standalone if needed), patient eligibility verification, enrollment workflows, care gap identification, quality tracking, CMS data reporting via FHIR APIs, G-code claim submission, compliance monitoring, and ongoing optimization. In Full Service tier, we also provide care coordination staffing. Your physicians and staff do what they already do — see patients, manage medications, order labs. We make sure every billable activity is captured, every outcome is tracked, and every dollar flows from CMS to your practice.

What if ACCESS underperforms or CMS changes the model?
Because InPursuit operates on a revenue share with zero upfront cost, you bear no financial risk. If ACCESS OAPs come in lower than projected, you haven't lost an investment — you just earn less. If CMS modifies the model, TETRA's architecture is designed for regulatory adaptability. And because we also optimize your existing FFS billing (AWV, CCM, RPM, TCM, BHI), you're generating incremental revenue even before ACCESS OAPs start flowing in July 2026.
How does Transitional Care Management (TCM) capture work?
TCM pays $165-$225 per eligible discharge, but most practices capture under 2% because they don't know when their patients are discharged. TETRA integrates with ADT (Admit/Discharge/Transfer) feeds from local hospitals, automatically identifies your patients' discharges, triggers a 2-business-day interactive contact (required by CMS), schedules the 7- or 14-day follow-up visit, and generates 99495/99496 documentation. Every hospital discharge becomes a revenue event instead of a missed opportunity.
Can I participate in ACCESS and still be in an ACO?
Yes. ACCESS is designed to complement ACO and other risk-bearing arrangements. CMS has stated that for 2026-2027, ACCESS OAPs will have no impact on ACO benchmark or performance year calculations (MSSP and ACO REACH). Starting in 2028, ACCESS expenditures will be included in ACO calculations. Risk-bearing entities can refer their aligned beneficiaries to ACCESS participants — and referring providers earn a new Co-Management Payment (approximately $100/year per beneficiary) for documented review of patient updates.
