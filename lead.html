Why LEAD Changes the Game

What prior ACO models got wrong. What LEAD fixes.

No Benchmark Rebasing

Prior models punished success by ratcheting benchmarks down every 3 years. LEAD offers a 10-year horizon with stable benchmarks. Invest in care delivery knowing the goalposts won't move.

Prospective Cash Flow

MSSP pays retrospectively after 18+ months. LEAD pays prospective capitation monthly. Your ACO has working capital from day one — no waiting for CMS reconciliation to fund operations.

Rural & Independent Access

Add-on payments (not subject to reconciliation) help rural and independent providers build ACO infrastructure. Lower beneficiary alignment minimums for practices new to accountable care.

Specialist Integration (CARA)

CMS-Administered Risk Arrangements let ACOs formally partner with specialists on episode-based care. CMS manages the data sharing and payment — removing administrative burden from both sides.

Medicaid Integration Pathway

Planning phase (March 2026 – Dec 2027) will identify two pilot states for ACO-Medicaid partnerships. First-ever CMS model to build a formal bridge between Medicare ACOs and Medicaid programs.

High-Needs Population Design

Improved risk adjustment and benchmarking for complex, dually eligible, and homebound patients. LEAD is designed to make it financially viable to serve the patients other models couldn't support.

Questions

Everything you need to know about LEAD.

What is the LEAD Model and how is it different from MSSP?
LEAD (Long-term Enhanced ACO Design) is CMS Innovation Center's newest ACO model, launching January 1, 2027. It differs from MSSP in three fundamental ways: (1) Prospective capitation — monthly per-beneficiary payments instead of retrospective reconciliation, giving ACOs predictable cash flow; (2) 10-year performance period with no rebasing — MSSP rebases benchmarks at agreement renewal, punishing ACOs that improved. LEAD locks benchmarks for a decade; (3) Two voluntary risk tracks — Global (100/100) and Professional (50/50), both with capitated payments. MSSP has five BASIC levels plus ENHANCED. LEAD also introduces CARA specialist contracting, rural add-on payments, and a Medicaid integration pathway that MSSP lacks entirely.
We're currently in ACO REACH. How do we transition to LEAD?
ACO REACH concludes at the end of 2026. LEAD is designed as its successor and CMS anticipates current REACH participants will be among the primary LEAD applicants. The application window opens March 2026, and LEAD begins January 1, 2027. NAACOS and other industry groups have urged CMS to ensure a smooth transition timeline. InPursuit's TETRA infrastructure provides data continuity across the model transition — your population health analytics, care gap tracking, and quality reporting carry forward without rebuilding. Start your discovery engagement now so infrastructure is deployed and operational before the REACH-to-LEAD handoff.
What does "no benchmark rebasing" actually mean for our bottom line?
In MSSP, every time your agreement renews (typically every 5 years), CMS recalculates your benchmark based on your improved spending. If you saved 5% in years 1-5, your new benchmark starts 5% lower — making it harder to generate savings in years 6-10. This "ratchet effect" is the single biggest complaint about MSSP. LEAD eliminates this entirely. Your benchmark pathway is set for 10 years. If you invest in care delivery infrastructure in Year 1 and start generating savings by Year 3, those savings compound through Year 10 without the goalposts moving. This is why the 10-year cumulative projections in our calculator show dramatically higher returns than a 5-year MSSP cycle — it's not optimistic modeling, it's the structural advantage of stable benchmarks.
What are CARA specialist contracting arrangements?
CMS-Administered Risk Arrangements (CARA) are a new feature unique to LEAD. They allow ACOs to establish episode-based risk-sharing arrangements with specialist providers without the administrative burden of building and managing those contracts independently. CMS handles the data sharing infrastructure, episode definition, and payment mechanics. ACOs share episode data with their preferred specialist providers, and payments flow based on episode performance. The initial use case is a falls prevention episode. InPursuit's TETRA Ex provides the bidirectional data exchange that CARA requires — episode cost tracking, specialist performance analytics, and the interoperability layer between your ACO and your specialist network.
Can we be in MSSP now and transition to LEAD later?
Yes — and this is the recommended path for many ACOs. Deploy TETRA infrastructure under MSSP today. Generate FFS revenue and shared savings immediately. Then when LEAD launches in 2027, your infrastructure, your care model, your data analytics, and your team's operational muscle are already built. The transition is a program switch, not a technology rebuild. TETRA is model-agnostic — it powers MSSP, ACCESS, LEAD, and any future CMS model from a single deployment.
We're a small, rural, or independent practice. Is LEAD for us?
LEAD was explicitly designed with you in mind. CMS has built in several accommodations: Rural add-on payments that are not subject to reconciliation — meaning you receive infrastructure funding regardless of savings performance. Lower beneficiary alignment minimums for providers new to ACO participation. Improved benchmarking designed to make participation viable for practices serving smaller or more complex populations.
