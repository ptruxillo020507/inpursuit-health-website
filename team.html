How TETRA Powers TEAM Performance

Five surgical episodes. Every dollar tracked.

TEAM holds hospitals accountable for total episode cost from surgery through 30 days post-discharge. TETRA provides real-time visibility into every cost driver.

Episode Cost Driver
What TETRA Does
Post-Acute Care Steering
SNF and home health costs are 30-40% of total episode spend. Facility selection drives outcomes.
TETRA™ aggregates SNF quality, readmission, and cost data to build preferred network scorecards. Discharge planners see real-time facility rankings. Home health routing algorithms identify patients who can safely recover at home instead of in a SNF.
Readmission Prevention
Surgical readmissions within 30 days are counted in the episode. Each costs $15,000+.
TETRA™ automates post-discharge follow-up workflows — TCM scheduling, medication reconciliation alerts, wound care check-in triggers, and patient-reported outcome collection. Flags high-risk patients for nurse navigator escalation before complications develop.
Complication Monitoring
Surgical site infections, VTE, and other post-op complications drive episode cost overruns.
TETRA Aegis™ monitors post-op vitals via RPM devices (temperature, wound photos, pulse ox) and flags early signs of complications for same-day clinical intervention. Catches infections at day 4, not day 14.
Quality Performance Adjustment
Reconciliation payments are adjusted by quality scores including patient-reported outcomes (PROs).
TETRA™ automates PRO collection via patient-facing digital surveys at pre-op and post-op intervals. Quality dashboards track every TEAM measure in real time. Higher quality scores = larger reconciliation payments.

Questions

Everything you need to know about TEAM.

Our hospital is in a mandatory CBSA. Do we have any choice?
No — participation is mandatory for all IPPS hospitals in the 188 selected CBSAs. You cannot opt out. However, you can choose your track: Track 1 (PY1 only, upside-only) gives you a one-year glide path before two-sided risk. Safety-net and rural hospitals can remain on Track 2 (reduced risk) for the full model. Use the PY1 glide path to deploy TETRA, build your post-acute network, and establish care navigation workflows before full financial risk begins in PY2.
How do target prices work?
CMS calculates hospital-specific target prices for each of the five episode categories based on three years of your hospital's baseline claims data, risk-adjusted for patient complexity and hospital characteristics. Target prices cover all Medicare Parts A and B costs from admission through 30 days post-discharge — including the index surgery, SNF stays, home health, readmissions, and follow-up visits. You continue billing Medicare FFS as usual. At annual reconciliation, CMS compares your actual episode costs to your target prices. If you're below, you earn a payment (adjusted for quality). If you're above, you may owe CMS (depending on track).
Where do most episode savings come from?
Research from BPCI Advanced and CJR consistently shows that post-acute care is 30-40% of total episode cost and the primary savings lever. Specifically: (1) SNF selection — steering patients to preferred facilities with shorter stays and lower readmission rates; (2) Home health diversion — identifying patients who can safely recover at home instead of in a SNF; (3) Readmission prevention — TCM follow-up within 14 days, medication reconciliation, and complication monitoring. The surgical index cost is relatively fixed. The post-acute tail is where the variability — and the savings — live. That's exactly where TETRA's data infrastructure makes the biggest impact.
Can we participate in TEAM and an ACO model simultaneously?
Yes. CMS has designed TEAM to complement ACO participation, not conflict with it. TEAM is episode-based (30-day surgical window); MSSP/LEAD are longitudinal (total cost of care, full year). CMS has published overlap policies ensuring hospitals can participate in both. InPursuit's infrastructure is model-agnostic — TETRA powers episode analytics for TEAM and population health for MSSP/LEAD from a single deployment.
How long does deployment take?
4-6 weeks. TEAM is already live. Every week without episode-level data visibility is a week of unmanaged costs. TETRA deploys alongside your existing EHR and begins surfacing episode cost drivers, post-acute utilization patterns, and readmission risk within the first month.
