Responsible AI

How We Use Artificial Intelligence —
And How We Don't

AI is a powerful tool for improving patient outcomes. But power demands responsibility. Here's exactly how InPursuit uses AI — and where we draw hard lines.

01

AI Identifies Care Gaps — Not Marketing Targets

Our AI analyzes clinical data to find patients overdue for annual wellness visits, flag uncontrolled chronic conditions, and recommend evidence-based interventions. It exists to close care gaps, not to profile consumers.

02

Your Data Trains Your Models — Nobody Else's

When AI learns from your patient population, those insights stay with your practice. We never pool your clinical data with other organizations to build products we sell elsewhere. Your data, your intelligence, your competitive advantage.

03

Clinicians Make Decisions — AI Informs Them

AI generates recommendations and surfaces patterns. It never overrides physician judgment, auto-prescribes treatments, or makes clinical decisions autonomously. The provider is always the quarterback.

04

Explainable, Auditable, Accountable

Every AI-generated recommendation includes the reasoning behind it. No black boxes. Providers can see why a patient was flagged, what data drove the alert, and how confidence scores were calculated.

05

Bias Monitoring & Fairness Checks

We continuously test our models for demographic bias in risk scoring, care recommendations, and patient outreach prioritization. Equitable care delivery isn't optional — it's engineered into the platform.

The TETRA AI Promise

Every line of AI code in the TETRA platform is governed by a single principle:

"Does this improve patient outcomes or reduce provider burden?"

If the answer is no, it doesn't ship.

What we will never do: Sell AI-derived insights, share anonymized data sets, license predictive models built on your patients, or allow third-party access to your clinical intelligence.
