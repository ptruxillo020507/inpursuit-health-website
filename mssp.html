MSSP Track Options

Two pathways. One infrastructure.

MSSP offers BASIC and ENHANCED tracks with varying risk/reward profiles. Starting January 2027, new ACOs are capped at 5 years in one-sided risk before mandatory escalation.

BASIC (One-Sided)
Up to 50% savings
No downside risk. Ideal for ACOs new to value-based care. MSR varies by population size (2.0-3.9%). Limited to 5 years starting 2027.
BASIC (Two-Sided)
50% savings / 40% losses
Levels C through E with escalating risk. Level E offers the maximum BASIC sharing rate with moderate downside exposure.
ENHANCED
75% savings / 40-75% losses
Maximum shared savings rate. Full two-sided risk. Prepaid shared savings option available starting PY 2026 for qualifying ACOs.
APP Plus Quality
6–11 measures (2025-2028)
All tracks must meet APP Plus quality performance standard. eCQM/MIPS CQM/Medicare CQM reporting mandatory. TETRA automates this entirely.

What's the difference between BASIC and ENHANCED tracks?
BASIC has five levels (A through E). Level A is one-sided risk (upside only, up to 40% sharing). Levels B-E escalate both sharing rates and downside exposure, with Level E at 50% savings / 40% losses. ENHANCED provides 75% shared savings but with 40-75% shared losses. Starting January 2027, new ACOs are capped at 5 years in one-sided risk before mandatory escalation to two-sided. The track you choose should reflect your risk tolerance, population size, and infrastructure readiness. InPursuit helps you model the financial scenarios for each track during discovery — and provides the infrastructure to perform regardless of which track you choose. Most ACOs with TETRA infrastructure graduate to ENHANCED faster because they have the data confidence to accept risk.
Can we participate in MSSP and ACCESS simultaneously?
Yes, with caveats. CMS has published guidance on model overlap: beneficiary alignment in a disease-specific CMMI model (like ACCESS) generally takes precedence over MSSP voluntary alignment for those specific patients. However, MSSP covers your total attributed population while ACCESS targets specific chronic condition tracks — so the overlap is limited to the subset of patients who qualify for both. InPursuit's infrastructure is model-agnostic: TETRA orchestrates data across all programs from a single deployment. You don't need separate systems. In fact, participating in both maximizes revenue because you capture ACCESS OAPs for qualifying patients AND shared savings from TCOC reduction across the broader population. We review your specific overlap during discovery.
How is MSSP affected by Medicare Advantage growth? Is FFS shrinking?
Yes — Medicare Advantage now exceeds 50% of total Medicare enrollment, and the FFS pool is shrinking. CMS knows this, which is why they're actively expanding MSSP incentives (prepaid shared savings starting PY 2026, expanded assignment criteria, lower barriers for new ACOs) and launching new models like LEAD (10-year capitated model starting 2027) and ACCESS (outcome-aligned payments starting 2026). MSSP is not going away in 5 years — CMS's stated goal is 100% of traditional Medicare in accountable care by 2030, and MSSP is their primary vehicle. But MSSP is also a bridge, not a destination. The strategic play: deploy TETRA infrastructure for MSSP today, and you're already wired for LEAD, ACCESS, and whatever CMS launches next. The infrastructure is model-agnostic — it moves with you.
What is the prepaid shared savings option?
New for PY 2026, CMS will offer prepaid shared savings (PSS) to qualifying ACOs with a strong history of earning savings (CY 2025 PFS Final Rule). PSS provides advance payments on expected shared savings to improve ACO cash flow — addressing the biggest complaint about MSSP: waiting 18+ months for reconciliation. These payments are reconciled against actual performance; if you don't earn sufficient savings, CMS may recoup from your repayment mechanism. InPursuit's reconciliation intelligence tools model your expected PSS eligibility and track real-time performance against advance payments so you never face a surprise clawback.
